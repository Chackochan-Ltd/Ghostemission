Understanding Ghost Emissions

What Are Ghost Emissions?

Ghost emissions are greenhouse gas leaks from abandoned or incomplete infrastructure that continue emitting long after projects are halted. Unlike operational facilities, these sources are rarely monitored or accounted for in climate inventories.

The Lavasa Case Study

Lavasa hill station, once envisioned as India's first planned hill city, was abandoned mid-construction. The incomplete sewage systems, disturbed soil, and decaying organic matter create perfect conditions for methane generation.

Hidden Climate Impact

Methane is 28 times more potent than CO‚ÇÇ over a 100-year period. The 2.8 MT of CO‚ÇÇ-equivalent emissions from Lavasa alone equal the annual emissions of 600 cars - completely invisible to current monitoring systems.

The Bigger Picture

Thousands of abandoned developments worldwide could be contributing significantly to global methane emissions. Better satellite monitoring and policy frameworks are needed to track and mitigate these "ghost" sources.

Next Steps

This analysis demonstrates the urgent need for systematic monitoring of abandoned infrastructure. We recommend:

  • Mandatory pre-abandonment environmental impact assessments
  • Satellite monitoring programs for known abandoned sites
  • Policy frameworks requiring cleanup of emission sources
  • Integration of ghost emissions in national climate inventories
